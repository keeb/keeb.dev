AI-Native Infrastructure

2026-02-03 — keeb

We are at an inflection point in how infrastructure is conceived, built, and operated. The old model—hand-authored Terraform plans, static YAML manifests reviewed in pull requests, bespoke Ansible playbooks—was designed for human operators reasoning about discrete, well-understood components. But the systems we build now are not discrete, and the pace at which they evolve has outstripped our ability to reason about them manually. It is time to rethink infrastructure from first principles, treating AI not as an afterthought bolted onto existing pipelines, but as a native participant in the provisioning, observability, and remediation loop.

AI-native infrastructure means the control plane itself is conversational. Instead of writing a resource "aws_instance" block by hand, you describe intent and constraints; the system synthesizes the appropriate plan, validates it against policy and cost guardrails, and executes—while keeping a human in the approval loop where it counts. The feedback cycle drops from hours to seconds, and the blast radius of any single change shrinks because the system understands dependencies holistically rather than through the narrow lens of a single module.

This is not about replacing engineers. It is about changing what engineers spend their time on: moving from the mechanical toil of config authoring to the higher-order work of defining policy, setting constraints, and designing the systems that design systems. The infrastructure-as-code era was a leap forward. The next leap is infrastructure-as-intent.
