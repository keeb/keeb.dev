[!] QUEST LOG: The Lore

Day 1 of the Journey // 2022-05-28

It began, as these things always do, with a blinking cursor in a dark room. There was no graphical interface to guide the way, no friendly wizard to point toward the next objective. Just a terminal prompt, patient and unblinking, waiting for input. I typed ls for the first time and watched the dungeon reveal its contents. Hidden directories. Mysterious config files. A whole filesystem stretching out beneath me like the procedurally generated floors of an endless dungeon. I had found the entrance, and there was no turning back.

The first real boss encounter was installing Linux on bare metal. Armed with nothing but a burned USB drive and a prayer, I descended into the BIOS configuration caverns and emerged three hours later with a working Ubuntu installation. Gained +1 to System Administration. The world looked different now. Every click, every window, every process was something I could inspect, modify, and break. And break things I did -- spectacularly, repeatedly, and with an enthusiasm that could only come from someone who hadn't yet learned to fear the rm -rf command.

Learning to code was less like reading a spellbook and more like stumbling through the dungeon of Stack Overflow, collecting fragments of arcane knowledge and stitching them together into something that occasionally compiled. Bash scripts became my first cantrips -- small, unreliable, but deeply satisfying when they worked. Gained +1 to Shell Scripting. Python followed, then JavaScript, each language a new branch in an ever-expanding skill tree. The experience points came slowly at first, but every solved bug, every passing test, every successfully parsed JSON blob felt like clearing a room full of hostiles.

[ ] SSH Key of Infinite Access

Building the first website was the culmination of countless side quests. The dungeon of DNS configuration nearly claimed me -- CNAME records, A records, nameserver propagation delays, all guarded by the final boss: a 48-hour TTL that made every mistake feel permanent. But the site went live. A single HTML page, served by a misconfigured Nginx instance, displaying "Hello, World" in a font I would later realize was Comic Sans. Gained +2 to Web Deployment. Gained +1 to Humility.

That was Day 1. The dungeon has only grown deeper since then. Every corridor leads to three more, every answered question spawns a dozen new ones, and the loot table of knowledge never seems to run dry. This quest log is my record of the crawl -- the bosses fought, the skills gained, the spectacular wipes and the hard-won victories. If you're reading this, adventurer, consider this your invitation. The dungeon is open. The cursor is blinking. Type your first command and begin.
