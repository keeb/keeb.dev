AI is great at parsing

Broadcast 2025-09-26 • 23:47 UTC

Good evening, night owls. You're tuned to KEEB.DEV FM and this is your host, coming at you live from somewhere between the kernel and userspace. Tonight's transmission is about something I keep running into—something that quietly changed how I work.

AI excels at understanding structure in ways that surprise you at three in the morning. Hand it a gnarly log dump, an undocumented API response, some half-broken XML from 2003—it doesn't flinch. Where you'd spend twenty minutes writing a regex that handles 90% of edge cases, a language model just reads the shape of the data and gets it. Not because it's magic, but because parsing is fundamentally about pattern recognition across messy, semi-structured input—exactly the territory where these models thrive.

The real unlock isn't replacing your parsers. It's that first pass—the exploratory phase where you're staring at data you've never seen before, trying to figure out what it even is. AI turns that from a chore into a conversation. You throw it a blob of text and ask "what is this?" and it tells you. It finds the fields, infers the hierarchy, spots the inconsistencies. It's like having a colleague who's read every RFC and every man page but never gets tired of looking at malformed JSON.
